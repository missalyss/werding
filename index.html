Each spring, the life force of Nature is once again restored. The warmth of the sun upon the soil, awakens the life within the seed. The cycle of regeneration begins anew, and from what appeared to be death comes new life. It seems that life is not extinguished by death, but that death brings new life. This reminds us that each death is only a transition. And with every transition, we learn that we must prepare the soil of our lives. We must plant the seeds of our hopes and desires. We must nurture those seeds, care for them and protect them if we wish to reap in the coming harvest.

In the case of this family, the only death is the idea of their separateness. They have known long before today that they are a family. Together, they have felt the strength of their community. They have known companionship, refuge, warmth, affection, and lots and lots of love.

Today, Brendon and Alyssa (with the help of Sophie) plant new seeds. By saying "I do", they affirm the commitment they have already known, the love they have already felt, and the family they already are. Love is a powerful force, a vicious cycle, where love begets more love. And with it, this family has been able, and will be able to reap connection, joy, and more love.
